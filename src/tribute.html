Imran Ahmad Khan Niazi

Imran Ahmad Khan Niazi is a Pakistani former cricketer and politician who served as the 22nd prime minister of Pakistan from August 2018 until April 2022.

About Imran

  • Imran Khan gained international fame as a cricketing legend, leading Pakistan to its first Cricket World Cup victory in 1992. His transition from a sports icon to a political leader showcases his versatility and ability to command respect in different arenas.
  • Khan's charisma has played a significant role in his political journey. His ability to connect with the masses through his speeches and public appearances has helped him garner a large following.
  • In 1996, Khan founded the PTI, a centrist political party with a focus on anti-corruption and social justice.
  • Khan's leadership emphasizes social justice and poverty alleviation.
  • Imran Khan's political narrative has heavily emphasized the eradication of corruption. He pledged to hold the powerful accountable for their misdeeds, tapping into the public's frustration with systemic corruption in Pakistan.
  • Imran Khan's leadership envisions a "Naya Pakistan" (New Pakistan) characterized by transparency, accountability, and good governance.
  • Khan's leadership has faced criticism for not fully delivering on some of his promises, including economic reforms and job creation.
  • Imran Khan's leadership has elevated Pakistan's global profile. He has spoken at international forums and raised awareness about issues like climate change and Islamophobia, positioning Pakistan as a voice on the global stage.
  • After years in the opposition, Imran Khan's PTI secured victory in the 2018 general elections, leading to his election as the 22nd Prime Minister of Pakistan.
